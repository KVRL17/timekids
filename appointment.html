Every class, group, and child is unique and has different needs at different times. At T.I.M.E. Kids pre-schools, we believe in a curriculum that matches abilities to skills that preschoolers need as a base for later learning and success at school. These can be broadly classified under eight headings:
  • Gross Motor Skills: Require balance and coordination, developed through activities such as running, climbing, hopping, and playing with ball and bat.
  • Fine Motor Skills: Help children develop the abilities to write and manipulate small objects, developed through activities like playing with dough, sorting, dressing, coloring, and craft.
  • Mathematical Concepts: Understood through activities that include matching objects, sorting and classifying by attribute, using comparative terms, labeling numerals (0-10), recognizing numbers (1-30), and rote reciting of numbers (1-100).
  • Science Concepts: Understood through activities that include observing and identifying body parts, using the five senses, colors, classifying and categorizing animals, identifying opposites, and learning about natural resources.
  • Language Concepts: Developed through activities that include listening, dramatic play, recalling story events, following verbal directions, matching and labeling letters, utilizing language to form questions, identifying letter-sound relationships, and recognizing and writing letters and words phonetically.
  • Life Skills: A diverse area where children are taught the importance of using positive manners in both words and attitudes, learning to follow directions, and solving problems verbally instead of using physical means, among others.
  • Music Skills: Help children acquire language and a sense of rhythm. It includes copying movements, moving physically to music, dancing, using creative movement, expressing mood through movement, and identifying instruments by name.
  • Art and Craft: A necessary part of a child's learning curriculum, including drawing, painting, pasting, tearing, cutting, rolling papers, rubbing, and mixing colors.

Interesting themes and concepts are covered at T.I.M.E. Kids pre-schools, making learning a unique, fun experience for the children. All events and festivals are celebrated with enthusiasm throughout the year. Life skills are a part of the "Curriculum" of learning experiences offered within a classroom setting at T.I.M.E. Kids pre-schools. The child learns to become a productive group member, solve problems, remember rules, and develop an understanding of cause and effect.

A playgroup for children aged 2 to 3 years is a fun and engaging environment where little ones begin their learning journey. At this stage, children explore new activities that help develop their social, emotional, physical, and cognitive skills. Through play, they learn to interact with peers, share, and express their feelings. They also engage in activities like storytelling, singing, and simple art projects that boost their creativity and language skills. Physical play helps improve coordination, while sensory activities stimulate curiosity. The playgroup fosters independence, confidence, and early learning, all in a nurturing and joyful space.

A nursery for children aged 3 to 4 years provides a structured yet playful environment that supports early learning and development. At this stage, children are more independent and eager to explore the world around them. The nursery encourages this curiosity through a variety of activities aimed at developing social, emotional, physical, and cognitive skills.

Children engage in more collaborative play with peers, learning to communicate, cooperate, and solve problems together. Language development flourishes through stories, songs, and conversations, while early literacy and numeracy skills are introduced through fun activities like puzzles, counting games, and drawing.

Pre-primary 1, for children aged 4 to 4.5 years, is a fun and engaging stage that helps prepare them for formal schooling. During this time, children start learning basic reading, writing, and counting skills through playful and structured activities. They also develop social skills by working in groups, sharing, and following instructions. Fine motor skills, like writing and cutting, are practiced through art and craft projects, while physical activities improve their coordination. This stage encourages independence, creativity, and a love for learning, helping children gain confidence as they get ready for the next step in their education.

Pre-primary 2, for children aged 4.5 to 5.5 years, focuses on preparing them for formal school. During this stage, kids learn to read simple words, write letters, and solve basic math problems like counting and adding. They practice working in groups, sharing, and following classroom rules, building social skills and emotional awareness. Fine motor skills, like writing and cutting, improve through creative activities, while outdoor play enhances physical coordination. Pre-primary 2 helps children become more independent and confident, giving them the tools they need for a smooth transition to primary school.

T.I.M.E. Kids pre-schools offer a day care facility for working parents. The day care provides a safe, nurturing, and stimulating environment for children while their parents are at work. Trained staff engage children in various activities, including playtime, story sessions, and creative arts, promoting learning and development. The facility also emphasizes healthy meals and snacks, ensuring that children receive proper nutrition throughout the day. With flexible hours and a focus on individual needs, the day care aims to support parents and provide children with a loving and enriching experience.
